FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about window cleaning, tinting and our Delray Beach, FL services.

How often should I have my windows professionally cleaned?

For most Florida homes, we recommend professional window cleaning every 2 to 3 months. Florida's climate, with its humidity, salt air near the coast and frequent rain, means windows accumulate grime and mineral deposits faster than in other states. Commercial properties in high-traffic or coastal areas often benefit from monthly cleaning to maintain a professional appearance.

What is window tinting and what are its benefits?

Window tinting involves applying a protective film to your glass that reduces heat, glare and UV radiation entering your home or business. In Florida's intense sun, window tinting can significantly lower your cooling costs, protect your furniture and flooring from UV fading, and improve the comfort of your interior spaces. It also enhances privacy without blocking your view.

Can you clean skylights and high-rise windows?

Yes! Window Cleaning Delray is fully equipped and trained to clean skylights, high-rise windows, multi-story commercial buildings and other difficult-to-reach glass surfaces. We use professional-grade equipment and follow strict safety protocols for elevated work. If your windows are hard to reach, we have the solution — just give us a call.

Do you offer glass restoration and scratch removal?

Yes, we offer professional glass restoration and window scratch removal services throughout Florida. Our technicians can remove surface scratches, hard water stains and mineral deposits that regular cleaning cannot address. We also provide graffiti removal from glass surfaces. In many cases, we can restore your windows to like-new condition without the need for expensive replacement.

What areas in Florida do you serve?

We serve all of Florida! While we are based in Delray Beach and serve Palm Beach County extensively, our team travels throughout the state for residential and commercial window cleaning, tinting and glass restoration projects.
